'''Shannon Brown''' (born November 29, 1985) is an American former professional [[basketball]] player. He attended [[Proviso East High School]] in [[Maywood, Illinois]], was named [[Illinois Mr. Basketball]] in 2003, and played college basketball for [[Michigan State University]]. He was drafted by the [[Cleveland Cavaliers]] with the 25th overall pick in the [[2006 NBA draft]].

==High school and college career==
Brown attended [[Proviso East High School]] in Maywood, where his teammates included fellow 2006 draftee [[Dee Brown (Illinois)|Dee Brown]]. In 2003, he was named [[Illinois Mr. Basketball]]<ref>{{Cite web |date=June 27, 2008 |title=Illinois Mr. Basketball first-round draft history |url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/2008/06/27/from-mr-basketball-to-mr-first-round/ |access-date=August 5, 2010 |website=[[Chicago Tribune]]}}</ref> and a [[McDonald's All-American]].

Considered a five-star recruit by [[Rivals.com]], Brown was listed as the No. 1 shooting guard and the No. 3 player in the nation in 2003.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Rivals |url=https://n.rivals.com/ |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=n.rivals.com}}</ref>

Brown played [[college basketball]] for the [[Michigan State Spartans men's basketball|Michigan State Spartans]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Shannon Brown College Stats |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/cbb/players/shannon-brown-1.html |access-date=2021-07-16 |website=College Basketball at Sports-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> He was second-team All-Big Ten as a junior and an All-Big Ten Defensive selection.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Shannon Brown Bio |url=http://www.nba.com/playerfile/shannon_brown/bio.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100809010045/http://www.nba.com/playerfile/shannon_brown/bio.html |archive-date=August 9, 2010 |access-date=August 5, 2010 |website=[[NBA]]}}</ref>

==Professional career==
===Cleveland Cavaliers (2006–2008)===
[[File:Shannon Brown Luke Walton.jpg|thumb|Brown (left) practices shooting with former Lakers teammate [[Luke Walton]].]]
Brown was drafted in the first round with the 25th pick of the [[2006 NBA draft]] by the [[Cleveland Cavaliers]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=2006 NBA Draft |url=https://www.basketball-reference.com/draft/NBA_2006.html |access-date=2021-07-16 |website=Basketball-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> Appearing in only 23 games (five starts) in [[2006-07 NBA season|his debut season]], he showed promise, scoring in double figures twice (10 points vs. [[New York Knicks|New York]] on November 29 and 14 vs. [[Toronto Raptors|Toronto]] on 3/3), but was hampered by an injury to his [[Shinbone|shin]].

Brown was assigned to the [[NBA Development League]]'s [[Albuquerque Thunderbirds]] on March 2, 2007,<ref>{{Cite web |date=March 2, 2007 |title=Cavaliers Assign Shannon Brown To Albuquerque Thunderbirds |url=http://www.nba.com/dleague/albuquerque/brown_070302.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100217142732/http://www.nba.com/dleague/albuquerque/brown_070302.html |archive-date=February 17, 2010 |access-date=November 3, 2010 |work=Nba.com}}</ref> but was recalled by the Cavaliers a day later. In his sole game as a Thunderbird, Brown scored 14 points with four rebounds and six assists.<ref name="assignedtovipers">{{Cite web |date=January 11, 2008 |title=Shannon Brown Assigned to Vipers |url=http://www.nba.com/cavaliers/news/brown_assigned_080111.html |access-date=November 3, 2010 |work=Nba.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Cavaliers Recall Shannon Brown |url=http://www.nba.com/dleague/albuquerque/brown_070303.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100217152142/http://www.nba.com/dleague/albuquerque/brown_070303.html |archive-date=February 17, 2010 |access-date=November 3, 2010 |work=Nba.com}}</ref> Brown returned to the [[NBA Development League]], this time with the [[Rio Grande Valley Vipers]], on January 11, 2008.<ref name=assignedtovipers/> In four games as a Viper, He averaged 23.5 points, including a 37-point performance against the [[Dakota Wizards]] on January 16.<ref>{{Cite web |date=November 27, 2009 |title=Vipers Overcome Wizards' Spell at D-League Showcase |url=http://www.nba.com/dleague/games/20080116/DAKRGV/recap.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121111090751/http://www.nba.com/dleague/games/20080116/DAKRGV/recap.html |archive-date=November 11, 2012 |access-date=November 3, 2010 |work=Nba.com}}</ref> He was recalled by the Cavaliers on January 17.<ref>{{Cite web |date=January 17, 2008 |title=Cavaliers Recall Brown from Vipers |url=http://www.nba.com/cavaliers/news/brown_recalled_080117.html |access-date=November 3, 2010 |work=Nba.com}}</ref>

Through [[2007-08 NBA season|2007–08]]'s [[All-Star break (NBA)|All-Star break]], Brown played in 15 games during the season (starting four games), averaging 7.0 points per game.

===Chicago Bulls (2008)===
On February 21, 2008, Brown was traded to the [[Chicago Bulls]] as part of a three-team deal between the Bulls, the [[Cleveland Cavaliers|Cavaliers]], and the [[Seattle SuperSonics|Sonics]]. The Sonics received Cavs forward [[Ira Newble]], Cavs forward [[Donyell Marshall]], and Bulls forward [[Adrian Griffin]]. The Cavs received Bulls center [[Ben Wallace (basketball)|Ben Wallace]], Bulls forward [[Joe Smith (basketball)|Joe Smith]], the Bulls' 2009 second round pick (who turned out to be [[Danny Green (basketball)|Danny Green]]), Sonics forward [[Wally Szczerbiak]], and Sonics guard [[Delonte West]]. While the Bulls received Brown, Cavs forward [[Drew Gooden]], Cavs guard [[Larry Hughes]], and Cavs forward [[Cedric Simmons]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=February 21, 2008 |title=Bulls acquire Gooden and Hughes in three-team trade |url=http://www.nba.com/bulls/news/hughes_gooden_080221.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080306200219/http://www.nba.com/bulls/news/hughes_gooden_080221.html |archive-date=March 6, 2008 |access-date=January 28, 2009 |website=[[NBA.com]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=February 22, 2008 |title=Cavs get Wallace from Bulls, Szczerbiak from Sonics – NBA – ESPN |url=https://www.espn.com/nba/news/story?id=3257159 |access-date=April 9, 2012 |publisher=[[ESPN]]}}</ref>

===Charlotte Bobcats (2008–2009)===
On August 6, 2008, Brown was signed to the [[Charlotte Bobcats]] to a one-year contract worth the minimum NBA salary of $800,000.<ref>{{Cite web |date=August 6, 2008 |title=Bobcats sign Shannon Brown |url=http://www.nba.com/bobcats/sign_shannon_brown_080806.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080905005057/http://www.nba.com/bobcats/sign_shannon_brown_080806.html |archive-date=September 5, 2008 |access-date=January 28, 2009 |website=[[NBA.com]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Associated Press Text size A A A |title=Bobcats sign G Shannon Brown to 1-year deal – NBA – Sporting News |url=http://aol.sportingnews.com/nba/story/2008-08-06/bobcats-sign-g-shannon-brown-1-year-deal?story-topic-NBA=Golf |access-date=April 9, 2012 |publisher=Aol.sportingnews.com}}</ref> He would average only 4.8 points in limited action with the Bobcats.

===Los Angeles Lakers (2009–2011)===
On February 7, 2009, Brown was traded, along with [[Adam Morrison]], to the Los Angeles Lakers for [[Vladimir Radmanović]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=February 7, 2009 |title=Lakers acquire adam morrison and shannon brown in exchange for vladimir radmanovic |url=http://www.nba.com/lakers/news/090207acquiremorrisonbrown.html |access-date=February 7, 2009 |website=[[NBA.com]]}}</ref>

Brown's playing time with the Lakers was initially limited. Towards the end of the season, Brown experienced an upswing of playtime. In the final five games of the season, Brown played for an average of 16.4 minutes. With those minutes, he averaged 7.2 points, 2.4 rebounds, and 1.6 assists.<ref>{{Cite web |date=November 29, 1985 |title=Shannon Brown Game By Game Page |url=http://www.nba.com/playerfile/shannon_brown/game_by_game_stats.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101118212754/http://www.nba.com/playerfile/shannon_brown/game_by_game_stats.html |archive-date=November 18, 2010 |access-date=November 3, 2010 |work=NBA.com}}</ref>

Brown's increased playtime carried on to the playoffs. In the opening game in the first round match-up against the Jazz, Brown played 22 minutes. He had nine points, three assists, two rebounds and a steal. He finished the series averaging 17.4 minutes, 7.2 points, 1.2 rebounds, 1.8 assists, and one steal per game.

On July 6, 2009, Brown agreed to return to the Lakers for two years and $4.2 million.

It was announced on January 18, 2010, that he was selected to compete in the [[2010 NBA All-Star Weekend#Slam Dunk Contest|Sprite Slam Dunk Competition]] in Dallas at the 2010 All-Star weekend. On the same day, Brown led the Lakers with 22 points in their 98–92 win against the [[Orlando Magic]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=The Game Happens Here |url=http://www.nba.com/games/20100118/ORLLAL/gameinfo.html |access-date=November 3, 2010 |work=NBA.com}}</ref> Brown participated in the dunk contest on February 13, 2010, but did not advance beyond the first round.<ref>{{Cite web |date=February 14, 2010 |title=Nate Robinson becomes 3-time dunk champion; Shannon Brown struggles |url=http://www.freep.com/article/20100214/SPORTS03/2140458/1055/Sports07/Nate-Robinson-becomes-3-time-dunk-champion-Shannon-Brown-struggles |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100217190559/http://www.freep.com/article/20100214/SPORTS03/2140458/1055/Sports07/Nate-Robinson-becomes-3-time-dunk-champion-Shannon-Brown-struggles |archive-date=2010-02-17 |access-date=2021-07-16 |website=Detroit Free Press}}</ref>
[[File:ShBrownLAL2010.jpg|thumb|right|upright|Brown with the Lakers in 2010]]
On February 16, 2010, in a game against the [[Golden State Warriors]], Brown scored a then career-high 27 points and grabbed 10 rebounds for his first career double-double.<ref>{{Cite web |title=The Game Happens Here |url=http://www.nba.com/games/20100216/GSWLAL/gameinfo.html |access-date=November 3, 2010 |work=NBA.com}}</ref> He won his [[2010 NBA Finals|second championship]] at the end of the season.

On August 8, 2010, Brown signed a two-year deal with the Lakers worth $4.6 million.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Shelburne |first=Ramona |date=August 5, 2010 |title=Agent: Shannon Brown, Lakers agree |url=https://www.espn.com/los-angeles/nba/news/story?id=5440224 |access-date=August 5, 2010 |website=[[ESPN]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=August 8, 2010 |title=Lakers re-sign shannon brown |url=http://www.nba.com/lakers/news/100811lakerssignshannonbrown.html |access-date=November 13, 2010 |website=[[NBA.com]]}}</ref> During that season, he averaged 8.7 points in 19 minutes per game. He scored a season-high 21 points in two separate games, against the [[Milwaukee Bucks]] (November 16) and the [[Chicago Bulls]] (November 23). During the postseason, Brown averaged seven points per game. In the Lakers' last two games against the [[Dallas Mavericks]], Brown scored 10 and 15 points respectively before his team was swept by the eventual champion.

On June 30, 2011, Brown elected not to exercise his option to extend his contract with the Lakers for the [[2011–12 NBA season|2011–12 season]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=June 30, 2011 |title=Los Angeles Lakers guard Shannon Brown becomes free agent |url=http://www.insidehoops.com/blog/?p=7924 |access-date=October 21, 2013 |website=InsideHoops.com}}</ref>

===Phoenix Suns (2011–2013)===
After the [[2011 NBA lockout]] ended on December 8, 2011, the [[Phoenix Suns]] signed Brown to a one-year contract.<ref>{{Cite web |date=December 10, 2011 |title=Suns Sign Shooting Guard Shannon Brown |url=http://www.nba.com/suns/news/brown_signed_111209.html |access-date=December 11, 2011 |website=[[NBA.com]]}}</ref> On March 15, 2012, Brown led the Suns with 21 points in a tough 91–87 victory over the [[Los Angeles Clippers]]. On March 27, 2012, Brown scored a career-high 32 points, with five 3-pointers, in a loss to the [[San Antonio Spurs]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Spurs vs. Suns – Game Recap – March 27, 2012 – ESPN |url=http://www.espn.com/nba/recap?gameId=320327021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181215174922/http://www.espn.com/nba/recap?gameId=320327021 |url-status=dead |archive-date=December 15, 2018 |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=ESPN.com}}</ref> On April 7, 2012, Brown scored 20 points in the third quarter against his former team, the [[Los Angeles Lakers]]. He led the team with 24 points in a blowout 125–105 victory. Two days later, Brown grabbed seven rebounds for the Suns (tied for the team game-high with [[Marcin Gortat]]) in a blowout 114–90 victory against the [[Minnesota Timberwolves]].

On July 25, 2012, Brown agreed to re-sign with Phoenix<ref>{{Cite web |title=Phoenix Suns News Headlines |url=https://www.nba.com/suns/news/brown_120725 |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=Phoenix Suns}}</ref> on a two-year deal worth $7 million. On November 7, 2012, Brown hit six straight three-pointers in the fourth quarter to lead the team in points with 24 and help them win 117–110 against his former team, the [[Charlotte Bobcats]]. Two days later, he scored 12 of his 22 points in the fourth quarter of a 107–105 victory against another former team, the [[Cleveland Cavaliers]]. This was the first time he scored 20 or more points in consecutive games.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Suns rally from 26 down to beat Cavaliers 107–105 |url=https://www.yahoo.com/news/suns-rally-26-down-beat-044315636--nba.html |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=www.yahoo.com}}</ref>

On October 25, 2013, Brown was traded, along with [[Marcin Gortat]], [[Malcolm Lee (basketball)|Malcolm Lee]], and [[Kendall Marshall]], to the [[Washington Wizards]] in exchange for [[Emeka Okafor]] and a 2014 first-round draft pick.<ref>{{Cite web |date=October 25, 2013 |title=Wizards Acquire Gortat From Suns |url=http://www.nba.com/wizards/wizards-acquire-gortat-suns |access-date=October 25, 2013 |website=NBA.com |publisher=Turner Sports Interactive, Inc.}}</ref> Brown, Lee, and Marshall were all waived by the Wizards three days later.<ref>{{Cite web |date=October 28, 2013 |title=Wizards Waive Brown, Lee and Marshall |url=http://www.nba.com/wizards/wizards-waive-brown-lee-and-marshall |access-date=October 28, 2013 |website=NBA.com |publisher=Turner Sports Interactive, Inc.}}</ref>

===San Antonio Spurs (2014)===
On February 1, 2014, Brown signed a 10-day contract with the [[San Antonio Spurs]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Spurs Sign Shannon Brown to 10-day Contract |url=https://www.nba.com/spurs/news/140201_spurs_sign_shannon_brown |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=San Antonio Spurs}}</ref> On February 12, 2014, he signed a second 10-day contract with the Spurs.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Spurs Sign Shannon Brown to Second 10-day Contract |url=https://www.nba.com/spurs/news/140212_shannon_brown_second_10_day_contract |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=San Antonio Spurs}}</ref> After Brown's second 10-day contract expired, the Spurs decided to part ways with him. The Spurs would eventually win the [[2014 NBA Finals|2014 Finals]] the same season, their 5th title in franchise history.

===New York Knicks (2014)===
On February 27, 2014, Brown signed a 10-day contract with the [[New York Knicks]].<ref>[http://knicksnow.com/posts/5272/knicks-sign-shannon-brown-earl-clark-to-10-day-contracts Knicks Sign Shannon Brown, Earl Clark To 10-Day Contracts] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140313210342/http://knicksnow.com/posts/5272/knicks-sign-shannon-brown-earl-clark-to-10-day-contracts |date=March 13, 2014 }}</ref> On March 10, 2014, he signed a second 10-day contract with the Knicks.<ref>[http://knicksnow.com/posts/5369/knicks-sign-earl-clark-and-shannon-brown-to-2nd-10-day-contracts Knicks Sign Earl Clark And Shannon Brown To 2nd 10-Day Contracts] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140910130131/http://knicksnow.com/posts/5369/knicks-sign-earl-clark-and-shannon-brown-to-2nd-10-day-contracts |date=September 10, 2014 }}</ref> On March 20, 2014, he signed with the Knicks for the rest of the season.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Knicks sign Shannon Brown for rest of season |url=http://www.insidehoops.com/blog/?p=15534 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141022122733/http://www.insidehoops.com/blog/?p=15534 |archive-date=October 22, 2014 |access-date=March 21, 2014 |df=mdy-all}}</ref> On July 23, 2014, he was waived by the Knicks.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Knicks waive Shannon Brown |url=http://www.insidehoops.com/blog/?p=16318 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141022121512/http://www.insidehoops.com/blog/?p=16318 |archive-date=October 22, 2014 |access-date=July 24, 2014 |df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=New York Knicks Cut Shannon Brown |url=http://www.knicks101.com/2014/07/23/new-york-knicks-cut-shannon-brown/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140728232133/http://www.knicks101.com/2014/07/23/new-york-knicks-cut-shannon-brown/ |archive-date=July 28, 2014 |access-date=July 28, 2014 |df=mdy-all}}</ref>

===Miami Heat (2014)===
On August 27, 2014, Brown signed with the [[Miami Heat]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=HEAT Signs Shannon Brown |url=https://www.nba.com/heat/news/heat-signs-shannon-brown |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=Miami Heat}}</ref> On November 24, 2014, he was waived by the Heat after appearing in five games.<ref>{{Cite web |title=HEAT Signs Hassan Whiteside |url=https://www.nba.com/heat/heat-signs-hassan-whiteside |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=Miami Heat}}</ref>

===Wisconsin Herd (2017–2018)===
On October 30, 2016, Brown was selected by the [[Grand Rapids Drive]] in the second round of the 2016 NBA Development League Draft,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Shannon Brown Drafted By Drive in Second Round |url=https://gleague.nba.com/news/shannon-brown-2016-nba-dleague-draft-grand-rapids-drive/ |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=NBA G League}}</ref> but was waived by the Drive on November 10.<ref>{{Cite web |date=November 10, 2016 |title=Drive Finalize Roster for Friday's Match-Up |url=https://www.oursportscentral.com/services/releases/drive-finalize-roster-for-fridays-match-up/n-5214688 |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=OurSports Central}}</ref>

On November 16, 2017, Brown was added to the roster of the [[Milwaukee Bucks]] G-League affiliate, the [[Wisconsin Herd]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=November 17, 2017 |title=Wisconsin Herd Adds NBA Veteran Shannon Brown |work=NBA.com |url=http://www.nba.com/bucks/news/wisconsin-herd-adds-nba-veteran-shannon-brown/ |access-date=November 16, 2017}}</ref>

===Delaware 87ers (2018)===
On January 26, 2018, Brown was traded to the [[Delaware Blue Coats|Delaware 87ers]], making his debut that same day.<ref>{{Cite news |date=January 28, 2018 |title=Delaware 87ers acquire two-time NBA champ Shannon Brown and Cameron Oliver |work=NBA.com |url=http://delaware.gleague.nba.com/news/delaware-87ers-acquire-two-time-nba-champ-shannon-brown-cameron-oliver/ |url-status=dead |access-date=January 27, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180126222427/http://delaware.gleague.nba.com/news/delaware-87ers-acquire-two-time-nba-champ-shannon-brown-cameron-oliver/ |archive-date=January 26, 2018 |df=mdy-all}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Brown has a son, Shannon Christopher Brown,<ref>{{Cite news |last=Sachs |first=Mark |date=December 25, 2009 |title=Shannon Brown |work=Los Angeles Times |url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-2009-dec-25-la-et-fave25-2009dec25-story.html}}</ref> and a daughter, Laiyah Shannon Brown, from his marriage to [[Monica (singer)|Monica]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=September 6, 2013 |title=Shannon and Monica Brown Welcome Daughter Laiyah Shannon |url=http://celebritybabies.people.com/2013/09/06/monica-shannon-brown-welcome-daughter-laiyah-shannon/ |access-date=September 21, 2013 |website=People |archive-date=September 26, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130926071826/http://celebritybabies.people.com/2013/09/06/monica-shannon-brown-welcome-daughter-laiyah-shannon/ |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In 2010, Brown began a relationship with [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] singer [[Monica (singer)|Monica]]. The pair met in June of that  year when Brown was consulted to star as her love interest in the music video for the single "[[Love All Over Me]]".<ref>{{Cite web |date=January 29, 2011 |title=Video: Monica – Love All Over Me |url=http://www.rap-up.com/2010/07/15/video-monica-love-all-over-me/#more-51996 |access-date=July 15, 2010 |website=[[Rap-Up]]}}</ref> In October 2010, Monica confirmed her engagement to Brown via [[Twitter]], posting a photo of her rose-cut diamond ring.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Hensel |first=Amanda |date=January 29, 2011 |title=Monica Engaged to Shannon Brown |url=http://music.aol.com/blog/2010/10/18/monica-engaged-shannon-brown/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101022172125/http://music.aol.com/blog/2010/10/18/monica-engaged-shannon-brown/ |archive-date=October 22, 2010 |access-date=October 18, 2010 |website=[[AOL Music]]}}</ref> On November 22, 2010, Monica and Brown were married in a secret ceremony at their Los Angeles home. The marriage, however, did not become a matter of public record until January 21, 2011, when Brown told the ''Hip-Hop Non-Stop TV-Show''.<ref>{{Cite news |date=January 21, 2011 |title=LA Lakers Shannon Brown Secretly Weds R&B Singer Monica |work=[[New York Post]] |url=http://www.nypost.com/p/entertainment/music/la_lakers_shannon_brown_secretly_hskFsfYR1wniyms9GxlH9H |access-date=January 21, 2011}}</ref> The couple had a second wedding ceremony for family and friends in July 2011.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Monica marries again in 'lavish' ceremony |url=http://marquee.blogs.cnn.com/2011/07/11/monica-marries-again-in-lavish-ceremony/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110714030023/http://marquee.blogs.cnn.com/2011/07/11/monica-marries-again-in-lavish-ceremony/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=July 14, 2011 |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=cnn.com}}</ref>

On March 27, 2019, it was reported Monica was divorcing Brown.<ref>{{Cite web |date=March 27, 2019 |title=Monica Ends It With Shannon Brown and Asks for a Divorce · Guardian Liberty Voice |url=https://guardianlv.com/2019/03/monica-ends-it-with-shannon-brown-and-asks-for-a-divorce/ |access-date=March 29, 2019 |website=guardianlv.com}}</ref> As of October 2019, their divorce was finalized.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Nelson |first=Jeff |date=October 22, 2019 |title=R&B Star Monica Finalizes Divorce from Shannon Brown: Report |url=https://people.com/music/monica-finalizes-divorce-shannon-brown/ |access-date=November 2, 2019 |website=[[People (magazine)|People]]}}</ref>

His brother [[Sterling Brown (basketball)|Sterling Brown]] is an American professional basketball player for [[KK Partizan|Partizan]] of the [[EuroLeague]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=STERLING BROWN {{!}} EuroLeague |url=https://www.euroleaguebasketball.net/euroleague/players/sterling-brown/012720/ |access-date=2024-04-09 |website=Euroleague Basketball |language=en-GB}}</ref>

===Legal issues===
On October 7, 2021, Brown was indicted for insurance fraud in the [[Southern District of New York]] for allegedly defrauding the NBA's health and welfare benefit plan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/crime-and-courts/nba-veterans-arrested-in-alleged-health-insurance-fraud-scheme-sources/3311071|title= 18 NBA Veterans Arrested in Alleged $4 Million Health Insurance Fraud Scheme |work=NBC News|date=October 7, 2021}}</ref> In April 2023, Brown consented to pay $320,000 in [[Restitution and unjust enrichment|restitution]] after pleading guilty to the charges.<ref>[https://www.yahoo.com/news/ex-nba-player-terrence-williams-120011861.html Ex-NBA player Terrence Williams helped defraud league of millions.
