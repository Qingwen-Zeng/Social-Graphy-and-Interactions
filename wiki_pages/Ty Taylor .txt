'''Ty Taylor''' (born January 5, 1969, in [[Montclair, New Jersey]]) is an American musician best known for being the lead singer of [[Los Angeles]] [[soul music|soul rock]] band [[Vintage Trouble]]. He is the voice of character Lester Grimes on the [[HBO]] series ''[[Vinyl (TV series)|Vinyl]]'' created by [[Martin Scorsese]] and [[Mick Jagger]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wmg.com/news/atlantic-and-warner-bros-records-create-groundbreaking-musical-experience-vinyl-new-hbo-drama|title=Warner Music Group Atlantic and Warner Bros. Records Create Groundbreaking Musical Experience for Vinyl, the New HBO® Drama Series from Martin Scorsese, Mick Jagger, and Terence Winter|website=Wmg.com|access-date=28 October 2021}}</ref>  Taylor has two songs, "[[Cha Cha Twist]]" and "[[The World Is Yours (Ty Taylor song)|The World Is Yours]]," featured on the 2016 [[soundtrack]] album ''Vinyl: Music from the HBO Original Series, Volume 1''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6843767/hbo-vinyl-soundtrack-exclusive|title=HBO's 'Vinyl' Series Soundtrack to Feature Otis Redding, Sturgill Simpson, David Johansen & Others: Exclusive|website=Billboard.com|access-date=28 October 2021}}</ref> He was formerly the guitarist and vocalist of [[R&B]] group [[Dakota Moon]] and was also a contestant on the [[reality TV show]] ''[[Rock Star: INXS]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cbs.com/primetime/rock_star/performers/ty/ |title=CBS - Rock Star |website=[[CBS]] |access-date=2006-05-23 |archive-date=2012-02-08 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120208075353/http://www.cbs.com/primetime/rock_star/performers/ty/ |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Music career==
[[Gospel music]] was a significant influence on Taylor, having sung in a [[Baptist Church]] [[choir]] in his youth. Other musical influences include [[Otis Redding]], [[Tina Turner]] and [[The Clash]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://artistwiki.com/ty-taylor/biography|title=Ty Taylor biography|website=Artistwiki.com|access-date=28 October 2021}}</ref>

After leaving ''Rockstar: INXS'', he was asked by [[Dave Navarro]] to sing guest lead vocals for his band [[Camp Freddy]]. In 2008, he formed the group [[Ghost Hounds]] and recorded a debut album, two songs from which were released as downloadable content for the video game ''[[Rock Band 2]]''.

He has toured with many artists including [[Tina Turner]], [[Eric Clapton]], [[Macy Gray]], [[Fiona Apple]], and Robert Taylor.

Taylor appeared on the cast recording of Jason Robert Brown's ''[[Songs for a New World]]'' recorded in 1996.

Taylor also appeared as a guest singer on the bonus album given away with ''Shout,'' recorded by [[Govt Mule]], on the song "Bring on the Music." Other guests included [[Steve Winwood]], [[Elvis Costello]], and [[Dr. John]].

Most recently, Taylor has been the front man for the [[Los Angeles]] [[soul music|soul rock]] band [[Vintage Trouble]]. Notable performances include a supporting performance at the final concert of the annual free Summer Concert Series at The Americana at Brand where Vintage Trouble opened for [[Paula Cole]] and [[Tyler Hilton]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/los-angeles/article/Grammy-winner-PAULA-COLE-Tyler-Hilton-and-Vintage-Trouble-close-Summer-Concert-Series-20010101|title=Grammy winner PAULA COLE, Tyler Hilton and Vintage Trouble close Summer Concert Series|website=BroadwayWorld.com|access-date=28 October 2021}}</ref> a headlining performance on the second stage of the California AIDS Walk (Taylor also performed [[Simon & Garfunkel]]'s "[[Bridge Over Troubled Water]]" on the main stage at the same benefit), and their weekly Tuesday night residency at Harvelle's Blues Club in [[Santa Monica, California]].

Vintage Trouble has supported rock acts like [[Lenny Kravitz]], [[Joss Stone]], [[Paloma Faith]], the [[Dixie Chicks]], [[The Who]], [[Bon Jovi]] and [[AC/DC]] on the 2015 [[Rock or Bust World Tour]].

==Musical theater==
Taylor studied at [[Carnegie Mellon University]] in [[Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania|Pittsburgh]], where he graduated with a [[Bachelor of Fine Arts]] degree in [[drama]] and [[music]]. In addition to his work with Dakota Moon and ''Rock Star: INXS'', Taylor's music career has included parts in Broadway [[musicals]] ''[[Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat]]'', ''[[Pippin (musical)|Pippin]]'',<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dailynews.com/ci_11517702 |title='Pippin's' Ty Taylor has magic to do - LA Daily News |publisher=Dailynews.com |date=2009-01-21 |access-date=2012-11-06 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110628211540/http://www.dailynews.com/ci_11517702 |archive-date=2011-06-28 }}</ref> ''[[Songs for a New World]]'', ''[[Grease (musical)|Grease]]'', and ''[[We Will Rock You (musical)|We Will Rock You]]''. Taylor also played the role of "Freddie" in a Los Angeles performance of the musical ''[[Chess (musical)|Chess]]'', written by [[Tim Rice]], [[Benny Andersson]] and [[Björn Ulvaeus]].<ref>[http://weblogs.variety.com/thesetlist/2007/08/index.html] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080329035244/http://weblogs.variety.com/thesetlist/2007/08/index.html|date=March 29, 2008}}</ref>

He also co-starred in the spoof ''[[Rogers the Musical]]'' with [[Adam Pascal]], featured in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] show ''[[Hawkeye (miniseries)|Hawkeye]]''. <ref>{{Cite web |last=Aslanian |first=Emily |title=How 'Rogers: The Musical' in 'Hawkeye' Came to Be |url=https://www.tvinsider.com/1024833/hawkeye-rogers-the-musical-marc-shaiman-scott-wittman-disney-plus/ |access-date=2022-09-18 |website=TV Insider |language=en-US}}</ref>
