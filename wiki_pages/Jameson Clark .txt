'''Jameson Clark''' (8 July 1907, in [[Kilbirnie]], [[North Ayrshire, Scotland|North Ayrshire]], Scotland – 4 January 1984, in Kilbirnie, Ayrshire, [[Scotland]]<ref name="bfiref">[https://web.archive.org/web/20090113205120/http://ftvdb.bfi.org.uk/sift/individual/71433 Year of birth and date of death]: [[British Film Institute|BFI.org.uk]] website. Retrieved 22 January 2008.</ref>) was a Scottish [[character actor]] who appeared in 22 films and made many appearances on television.

==Career==
His first appearance in a major film was the British production ''[[Whisky Galore! (1949 film)|Whisky Galore!]]'' (1949), playing Constable Macrae, a role he would reprise in the sequel, ''[[Rockets Galore! (film)|Rockets Galore!]]'' (1957)<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0042040/ ''Whisky Galore! (1949)''], as Constable Macrae: [[Internet Movie Database|IMDB.com]] website. Retrieved 22 January 2008.</ref> His early roles were all to a Scottish [[typecasting (acting)|typecast]], and he made 13 films, including ''[[The Maggie]]'' (1954) and ''[[X the Unknown]]'' (1956) in which he portrayed Scottish characters, before he was given the part of an English [[tugboat]] captain in ''[[The Key (1958 film)|The Key]]'' (1958).<ref name="imdbmaindetails">[https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0164050/maindetails Filmography details]: IMDB.com website. Retrieved 22 January 2008.</ref>

He appeared as McDougal in [[Ralph Thomas]]'s re-make of [[John Buchan, 1st Baron Tweedsmuir|John Buchan]]'s ''[[The 39 Steps (1959 film)|The 39 Steps]]'' (1959), which also starred [[Kenneth More]] as [[Richard Hannay]].<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0053354/ ''The 39 Steps (1959)''], as McDougal: IMDB.com website. Retrieved 22 January 2008.</ref> The last of his four films released in 1959 was [[Charles Crichton]]'s ''[[The Battle of the Sexes (1959 film)|The Battle of the Sexes]]'', playing Andrew Darling, with [[Peter Sellers]] and [[Robert Morley]] heading the cast.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20090115093738/http://ftvdb.bfi.org.uk/sift/title/144500 ''The Battle of the Sexes (1959)''], as Andrew Darling: BFI.org.uk website. Retrieved 22 January 2008.</ref>

After playing Sir Alan in the film ''[[Tunes of Glory]]'' (1960),<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0054412/ ''Tunes of Glory (1960)''], as Sir Alan: IMDB.com website. Retrieved 22 January 2008.</ref> he portrayed the Constable in ''[[Greyfriars Bobby: The True Story of a Dog]]'' (1961), a film based on the novel by [[Eleanor Stackhouse Atkinson|Eleanor Atkinson]], which recounts the tale of the [[Skye Terrier]] who would not leave his master's grave.<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0054944/ ''Greyfriars Bobby: The True Story of a Dog (1961)''], as the Constable: IMDB.com website. Retrieved 22 January 2008.</ref>

Clark briefly appeared on television, playing Jamie Lang in an episode of the British police series ''[[No Hiding Place]]'' ("Whistle and I'll Come", 1961).<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20090205012350/http://ftvdb.bfi.org.uk/sift/title/809378?view=cast "Whistle and I'll Come"], ''No Hiding Place'', 1961: BFI.org.uk website. Retrieved 22 January 2008.</ref> After playing George Lockwood in the film ''[[A Pair of Briefs]]'' (1962),<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20090116042637/http://ftvdb.bfi.org.uk/sift/title/45649?view=cast ''A Pair of Briefs'', as George Lockwood: BFI.org.uk website. Retrieved on January 22, 2008.]</ref> Clark returned to TV as a Foreman in an episode of the ''[[Suspense (UK TV series)|Suspense]]'' anthology series ("Diversion to Danger", 1963).<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0714137/ "Diversion to Danger"], ''Suspense'', 1963: IMDB.com website. Retrieved 22 January 2008.</ref> He would remain on the small screen for the next six years, in such series as ''[[The Troubleshooters (British TV series)|The Troubleshooters]]'' and ''[[Dr. Finlay|Dr. Finlay's Casebook]]'', also re-surfacing in ''No Hiding Place'', this time as Harry Armstrong in the episode "All Dead and Buried" (1963).<ref name="imdbmaindetails" />

Clark's final film was ''[[Ring of Bright Water (film)|Ring of Bright Water]]'' (1969), playing the Storekeeper in the film about otters based on the book by the naturalist [[Gavin Maxwell]].<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0064893/ ''Ring of Bright Water (1969)''], as the Storekeeper: IMDB.com website. Retrieved 22 January 2008.</ref> From then until his retirement in 1980, he concentrated once more on contributions to television projects, amongst which were ''[[Sutherland's Law]]'', ''[[The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie (novel)|The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie]]'', ''[[Play for Today]]'' and ''[[An Enemy of the People#Adaptations|Enemy of the People]]''.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20090114105708/http://ftvdb.bfi.org.uk/sift/individual/71433?view=credit Filmography]: BFI.org.uk website. Retrieved 22 January 2008.</ref>

Clark also appeared as Campbell McNair, an important Member of the Community, in an Episode of Dr Finlay's Casebook Episode 3 Season 2 broadcast 19th September 1963. 

Clark died on 4 January 1984 aged 76.<ref name="bfiref" />
