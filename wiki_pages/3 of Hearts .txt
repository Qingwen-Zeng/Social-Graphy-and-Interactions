'''3 of Hearts''' was an American [[country music]] trio composed of Blaire Stroud, Katie McNeill, and Deserea Wasdin, all natives of [[Fort Worth, Texas]]. Signed to [[RCA Records Nashville|RCA Nashville]] in 2001, the trio released its debut album in July of that year. Two of its singles entered the U.S. ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] charts, as did their rendition of "[[The Christmas Shoes (song)#Cover versions and other media|The Christmas Shoes]]."

==Biography==
Blaire Stroud, Katie McNeill, and Deserea Wasdin first sang at a funeral; They were also students at [[L.D. Bell High School]]; later, they cut a [[demo tape]] which was eventually sent to [[RCA Records Nashville|RCA Nashville]], who signed the trio to a record deal in 2000.<ref name="allmusic"/> They recorded the song, "Just Might Change Your Life", which appeared in the film and on the soundtrack for the film, "Where the Heart Is", in the Summer of 2000. 3 of Hearts also embarked on a tour sponsored by ''[[Seventeen (American magazine)|Seventeen]]'' magazine, in which the trio performed at shopping malls and promoted prom-related fashion trends.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p484989|pure_url=yes}} |title=3 of Hearts biography |access-date=2007-12-26 |last=Cohoon |first=Rick |work=[[Allmusic]]}}</ref>

Their debut album, also titled ''3 of Hearts'', was released in March 2001. Two of its singles ("Love Is Enough" and "Arizona Rain") entered the U.S. ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] charts, peaking at No.&nbsp;43 and No.&nbsp;59 respectively. However, 3 of Hearts did not enter the Top 40 on the country music charts until early 2002, when their rendition of "[[The Christmas Shoes (song)|The Christmas Shoes]]" reached a peak of No.&nbsp;39.
